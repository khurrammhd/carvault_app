CarVault Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 10, 2026

CarVault ("the app") is a mobile app for photographing and organizing vehicle documents (registration certificates and related paperwork). This page explains what data the app collects, why, and how you're in control of it.

Who runs this

CarVault is developed by an independent developer, contactable at khurram.mhd@gmail.com for any question about this policy or your data.

What the app collects

  • Account email address — collected when you sign in, via either email/password or Google Sign-In (Firebase Authentication). Used only to identify your account and secure your data; never used for marketing.
  • Vehicle and document data you enter — registration number, make, model, year, category (Buy/Sell), notes, and photos of documents you capture with the camera or choose from your gallery. This stays on your device by default, stored in the app's private sandboxed storage.
  • Google Drive backup (optional, off by default) — if you turn on backup in Settings, the same vehicle/document data and images are uploaded to a "CarVault Backups" folder created in your own Google Drive. The app only requests access to files it creates itself (Google's drive.file permission scope) — it cannot see, read, or modify any other file in your Drive. This data is stored under your Google account, subject to your own Google Account settings and storage quota, not on any server we operate.
  • Camera access — used only when you actively choose to photograph a document. The app does not access the camera in the background.
  • Basic crash/diagnostic data — via Firebase Crashlytics, in published builds only, to help fix bugs. This includes device model, OS version, app version, and the technical details of any crash — not your documents or personal data.

What the app does not do

  • No advertising and no ad-tracking SDKs.
  • No analytics or behavioral tracking beyond crash diagnostics above.
  • No selling, renting, or sharing your data with third parties.
  • No access to your Google Drive beyond the single backup file/folder the app itself creates.

Your control over this data

  • Turn Google Drive backup off, or disconnect your Google account, anytime from Settings.
  • Delete a vehicle or document at any time from within the app.
  • Delete the app to remove all data stored on your device.
  • Delete your Drive backup file directly from Google Drive, independent of the app.
  • Request deletion of your account and any associated data by emailing khurram.mhd@gmail.com.

Data security

Local data lives in the app's private, sandboxed storage on your device. Sign-in is handled by Firebase Authentication. Backup data, when enabled, is transmitted over an encrypted connection directly to Google Drive, authenticated as you.

Children's privacy

CarVault is not directed at children and is not knowingly used to collect data from anyone under 13.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the "Last updated" date above will change accordingly. Material changes will be reflected in the app's release notes.
